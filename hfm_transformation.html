Problem Statement

Consider a global finance organization with more than 2,000 users relying on Oracle HFM for financial consolidation and reporting. Every month (especially during close) FP&A managers and analysts manually extract, validate, and reconcile data to meet tight executive reporting deadlines.

For the FP&A manager, it means late nights during close. For the analyst, it means repetitive data pulls and version confusion. For senior management, it means delayed or inconsistent reporting. For the CFO, it means limited confidence in real-time performance visibility.

The result: valuable time spent gathering and validating data instead of analyzing it, increased risk of inconsistencies across regions, and limited visibility into real-time performance across the enterprise.

System Overview

Hyperion Financial Management is a product by Oracle that is used to report on actuals. The system is accessed by users mostly via Excel, where an add-on called SmartView allows for retrieval of data from the system.

Expected Outcome

A globally accepted reporting solution that standardizes reporting and self service analytics.

Key Challenges

Dashboard cannot tap into an Excel SmartView retrieve. Hierarchy structure for more than 2,000 legal entities is quite complex. To retrieve data, the user must provide values for eleven parameters. Data is then retrieved via a formula that points to the parameters.

Solution Architecture

Development

Hierarchy for entities and accounts was flattened via two dataflows, then connected to a bulk extract of the system that pulls in [Eliminations]. A complex but robust logic was applied to take into account Eliminations at the parent level and aggregate up in the hierarchy ensuring that numbers are not double counted.

Business Impact

  • - Standardized financial reporting at the WHQ FP&A level.
  • - Reduced manual data preparation during close cycles.
  • - Enabled governed self-service analytics for global FP&A users.
  • - Shifted reporting focus from data retrieval to performance analysis.
