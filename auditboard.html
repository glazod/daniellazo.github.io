System Overview

AuditBoard is a cloud ERP used by Controllership and Internal Audit to track issues raised from SOX and Audit reviews.

Problem Statement

Controllers have limited visibility into open overdue issues.

Expected Outcome

Controllers are able to track aged overdue issues by aging bucket and segment.

Key Challenges

  • Connection to database limited to DirectQuery mode
  • Inability to create SQL views or transform data before ingestion
  • No data dictionaries or data catalog available for reference
  • Tight five-week timeline with 1 FTE / 100 available development hours
  • Aging calculation and allocation buckets requires pre-alignment from the business

Solution Architecture

Development Approach

Development began with structured data exploration and alignment with business stakeholders to clearly define aging logic, ownership attribution, and reporting expectations. Given the absence of a data catalog and the constraints of DirectQuery mode, establishing semantic clarity across system fields was critical.

A comprehensive data mapping exercise was conducted to translate user interface terminology into underlying database structures. This resulted in the creation of a functional data dictionary, ensuring consistent interpretation of issue status, due dates, ownership, and remediation progress.

The solution architecture prioritized long-term sustainability. Rather than embedding hard-coded groupings or static logic (e.g., entity-to-region mappings), the model was designed to minimize future maintenance as organizational structures evolve. This approach reduced dependency on manual formula updates and preserved downstream calculation integrity.

Business Impact

  • Saved approximately 1,600 hours annually by eliminating manual issue tracking and reconciliation processes.
  • Generated an estimated ~$48,000 in annual cost savings through automation and standardized reporting.
  • Provided Controllers and Audit leadership with real-time visibility into overdue issues across entities and segments.
  • Standardized aging logic and ownership attribution, reducing ambiguity in remediation accountability.
  • Improved governance by introducing a structured remediation responsibility model, replacing fragmented segment ownership.
  • Established a scalable data model capable of adapting to organizational changes without recurring manual rework.
