Executive Summary

The Internal Audit Walkthrough Wizard is an AI-powered assistant deployed within Microsoft Teams to support Finance and Operations auditors during control walkthrough preparation. The solution leverages enterprise policy documentation and SOX control data to generate structured, contextual guidance in seconds â€” reducing administrative burden and improving audit consistency.

System Overview

The agent integrates with a centralized SharePoint policy repository and utilizes automated flows to retrieve the latest SOX control inventory from a structured online dataset. Based on user-selected audit areas, the solution surfaces applicable controls and generates walkthrough questions aligned to associated risks and policies.

The architecture intentionally separates deterministic control retrieval from generative AI question development to maintain reliability and governance.

Problem Statement

Staff auditors are routinely assigned new entities and multiple in-scope areas each month. For every walkthrough, they must manually identify applicable controls, reference relevant policies, and draft structured questions â€” a repetitive and time-consuming administrative process.

For the auditor, this meant lost preparation time. For audit leadership, it meant inconsistent walkthrough quality across regions. For the organization, it created variability in control execution and documentation rigor.

A scalable, governed AI solution was needed to standardize walkthrough preparation while preserving audit quality and control accuracy.

Expected Outcome

Deploy an AI-enabled assistant capable of delivering relevant controls, associated risks, policy references, and structured walkthrough questions within seconds â€” enabling auditors to focus on analysis rather than administrative preparation.

Key Challenges

  • Ensuring AI-generated output remained accurate and consistent within a controls-driven environment.
  • Maintaining governance and traceability over which controls were surfaced.
  • Balancing automation with audit reliability and regulatory expectations.

To address model trust concerns, a structured user selection framework was implemented. Controls are retrieved deterministically through automated workflows, while generative AI is used solely for contextual question development â€” ensuring consistency and minimizing hallucination risk.

Solution Architecture

The solution integrates Microsoft Teams, SharePoint document repositories, automated data flows, and Copilot Studio AI capabilities into a governed orchestration layer. Deterministic control mapping is executed via workflow automation, while AI augmentation generates contextualized walkthrough prompts.

Development Approach

The solution was designed, developed, tested, and deployed over a six-week period. User testing focused on consistency, clarity of control mapping, and relevance of generated walkthrough prompts.

The final deployment enabled auditors to interact directly with the assistant through Microsoft Teams without requiring additional training or system access.

Business Impact

  • Reduced walkthrough preparation time by approximately 30 minutes per audit area.
  • Standardized control identification across regions and audit teams.
  • Improved consistency in walkthrough documentation and questioning structure.
  • Freed auditor capacity to focus on risk analysis and value-added insights.
  • Established a foundation for scalable AI adoption within Internal Audit.

Next Steps

As agentic capabilities mature, the Walkthrough Wizard is positioned for migration to a more advanced Copilot web-based framework with expanded prompt orchestration, enhanced retrieval mechanisms, and deeper integration into audit workflows.
